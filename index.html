How scoring works

Your line is resampled and its curvature measured at every half metre. A simulated sports car (~1.1 g of grip, 198 km/h top speed) then drives it as fast as physics allows: cornering speed is limited by grip and curve radius, and braking/acceleration share grip with cornering via the friction circle. Tight radii force low speeds — the classic racing line trades a slightly longer path for much straighter, faster arcs.

The optimal line is computed by a minimum-curvature solver and scored by the exact same simulation, so the comparison is fair.
